OUTDOOR PLANTS

Outdoor plants are a bit different from indoor plants. They need to be able to withstand the elements, so they need to be tougher. Outdoor plants will not thrive if they are kept indoors. Plus, outdoor plants require more maintenance than indoor plants. Caring for outdoor plants is different from indoor plants. Outdoor plants need to be watered more often because they are exposed to the elements in the morning so they have all day to absorb the water. Check the soil before watering to see if it is dry. If it is, then water your plant. Fertilize your plants every two weeks with a balanced fertilizer. Deadhead spent flowers to encourage new growth. Pruning is also important for outdoor plants. Trim back dead or dying branches to encourage new growth.
